Functional Utilities

Definitions

Right to Left Evaluation

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} f \triangleleft x &= f(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Left to Right Evaluation

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} x \triangleright f &= f(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Regarding Left/Right Evaluation Operators

Think of the arrows as denoting the order of evaluation E.g. pass this term to the function on the left, or pass this term to the function on the right of the arrow. Essentially, this is useful for reducing parentheses.

Example

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \sin \triangleleft \frac{4}{12}\tau \;\displaystyle{\text{rad}} &= -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \\ \sin \left(\frac{4}{12}\tau \;\displaystyle{\text{rad}} \right) &= -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Right to Left Function Composition

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} f \circ g \triangleleft x &= f(g(x)) \\ &= f \triangleleft g \triangleleft x \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Derivative Shorthand

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta f(x) &= \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x} f(x) = f^\prime(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

For this notation, the derivative with respect to a given variable, is implicit.

Radians & Radians Conversion

Definitions

Constants

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \tau &= 2\pi = 360^{\circ} \\ \pi &= \frac{1}{2}\tau = 180^{\circ} \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Conversion

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} &\text{given}\\ 1^{\circ} &= \frac{1}{360} \tau \; {\displaystyle {\text{rad}}} \\ 1 \; rad &= \frac{1}{\tau} \cdot 360^{\circ}\\\\ &\text{therefore}\\ x^{\circ} &= \frac{x}{360} \tau \; {\displaystyle {\text{rad}}} \\ x \; rad &= \frac{x}{\tau} \cdot 360^{\circ} \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Limits

Vertical Asymptote

The line \(x=c\) is called a vertical asymptote of the curve \(y = f(x)\) if at least one of the following statements is true:

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \lim_{x \to c} f(x) &= + \infty \\ \lim_{x \to c} f(x) &= - \infty \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \lim_{x \to c^{-}} f(x) &= + \infty \\ \lim_{x \to c^{-}} f(x) &= - \infty \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \lim_{x \to c^{+}} f(x) &= + \infty \\ \lim_{x \to c^{+}} f(x) &= - \infty \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Horizontal Asymptote

The line \(y=L\) is called a horizontal asymptote of the curve \(y = f(x)\) if either of the following is true:

\( \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L \)\( \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = L \)

Limit Formulas

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \lim_{x \to c} \left( f \circ g \right)(x) &= f \Big( \lim_{x \to c} g(x) \Big) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Definitions

Standard Formula for Point \((\textcolor{cyan}{x_1}, \textcolor{cyan}{y_1})\)

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} P &= (\textcolor{cyan}{x_1}, \textcolor{cyan}{y_1}) \\ y - \textcolor{cyan}{y_1} &= f^{\prime}(\textcolor{cyan}{x_1})(x - \textcolor{cyan}{x_1}) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Alternative Formula at Point \(x = \textcolor{cyan}{a}\)

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} P &= (\textcolor{cyan}{a}, f \triangleleft \textcolor{cyan}{a}) \\ y &= f(\textcolor{cyan}{a}) + f^{\prime}(\textcolor{cyan}{a})(x - \textcolor{cyan}{a}) \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Function Reference

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \sin(x) &= \cos(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \cos(x) &= -\sin(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \tan(x) &= \sec^2(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \csc(x) &= -\cot(x)\csc(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \sec(x) &= \tan(x)\sec(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \cot(x) &= -\csc^2(x) \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \arcsin(x) &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \;\; \text{for all} -1 \leq x \leq 1 \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \arccos(x) &= - \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \;\; \text{for all} -1 \leq x \leq 1 \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta \arctan(x) &= \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \end{split} \end{equation}$$

Note

$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} |x| &= \sqrt{x^2} \neq x \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} |x|^2 &= x^2 \end{split} \end{equation}$$
$$\begin{equation} \begin{split} \delta |x| &= \frac{|x|}{x} = \frac{x}{|x|} \end{split} \end{equation}$$
